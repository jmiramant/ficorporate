EverFi Recognizes

Financial Literacy Month

As Financial Literacy Month arrives each April, headlines paint a bleak picture of the state of financial literacy in the United States. And many of the indicators are not good.

Despite the best efforts of educators, the financial preparedness resources available to students are inadequate. Currently, fewer than 20 US states mandate that high school students complete a course in financial literacy before high school graduation. Add to that the fact that only 26% of parents feel well prepared to teach their kids about basic personal finance.

But We’ve Got a Lot of Hope

In the world of Twitter and Facebook, Millennials look online for just about everything. At EverFi, we use digital learning and gaming to empower students with the essentials to make smart financial decisions.

This April, we all have an opportunity to make the headlines POSITIVE.

Future of Your Students

Saving, budgeting, or even earning money may not be top of mind for most teens, yet the financial knowledge they have now will set the tone for the decisions they make in their future. Today’s youth lack the basic understanding of personal finance that is necessary for future financial achievement, confidence, and well-being. In this economy, teaching personal finance at an early age is more important than ever.
