Financial Literacy Month

As Financial Literacy Month arrives each April, headlines paint a bleak picture of the state of financial literacy in the United States. And many of the indicators are not good.

Despite the best efforts of educators, the financial preparedness resources available to students are inadequate. Currently, fewer than 20 US states mandate that high school students complete a course in financial literacy before high school graduation. Add to that the fact that only 26% of parents feel well prepared to teach their kids about basic personal finance.

But We’ve Got a Lot of Hope

In the world of Twitter and Facebook, Millennials look online for just about everything. At EverFi, we use digital learning and gaming to empower students with the essentials to make smart financial decisions.

Join our Financial Literacy Mission.

It’s time for the public and private sectors to work together to address this issue head on. EverFi has partnered with hundreds of banks, companies, and foundations across the United States to bring new-media financial literacy education to over 5,000 schools. We’ve certified 5.5 million students in critical skills that empower their future. And we can proudly say that 98% of our teachers recommend this course to other educators.

This April, we all have an opportunity to make the headlines POSITIVE.

Empower the Financial Future of Your Students

The EverFi Financial Literacy Platform begins in elementary school and includes a dedicated high school module. This interactive, new media program was specifically designed to introduce financial literacy skills early in a child's cognitive development and empower students to cultivate good financial habits that will stay with them throughout their lives.

Most importantly, we partner with socially responsible organizations that are committed to supporting financial education, allowing us to offer this entire learning platform free to schools and students.
